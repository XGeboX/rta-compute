# Code of Conduct

This project is a small, public instrument built on an old tradition and
a modern standard of proof. The conduct expected here follows from that.

## The standard

**Argue from evidence.** Claims are settled by a source, an ephemeris,
or a reproducible computation, never by seniority, volume, or conviction.
A demonstrated wrong number outranks any opinion in the room, including
the maintainer's.

**Respect the tradition and the people.** Jyotiṣa is a living lineage and
a devotional practice for many who pass through here. Engage it, and each
other, with precision and good faith. Disagree with the math freely;
never with contempt for a person.

**Correct cleanly.** When you are wrong, say so and fix it. When someone
else is wrong, show the evidence and move on. The fix is the apology.

**Keep the walls.** Do not bring real people's birth data, private
charts, or editorial content into a public computational repository.
Protecting what is private is part of conducting yourself well here.

## Unacceptable

Harassment, demeaning remarks, or discrimination of any kind. Bad-faith
argument, deliberate misrepresentation of sources, or pressure to merge
unverified claims. Sharing another person's private astrological or
personal data without consent.

## Enforcement

Concerns may be raised privately with the maintainer at the contact in
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). Reports are handled discreetly. The
maintainer may edit, decline, or remove contributions, and may restrict
participation, to keep the project true to the standard above.
